Frequently Asked Questions

What is a podcast show notes formatter? ▾
A podcast show notes formatter takes your episode details — guest info, topics, timestamps, and resources — and formats them into ready-to-paste show notes for your publishing platform.
What platforms does this show notes generator support? ▾
It generates show notes in three formats simultaneously: HTML for WordPress and other CMS platforms, Markdown for Substack and Ghost, and Plain Text for Spotify and Apple Podcasts description fields.
Do I need to create an account to use this tool? ▾
No. The tool is completely free and requires no account or sign-up. Your template data is saved in your browser's local storage only — nothing leaves your device.
Can I save my intro and CTA as a template? ▾
Yes! Click 'Save as Template' to store your show intro and call-to-action in local storage. They will be pre-filled automatically the next time you open the tool — perfect for recurring podcast formats.
What are podcast show notes timestamps? ▾
Timestamps are chapter markers — like [00:00] Intro, [05:30] Guest background — that help listeners navigate to specific parts of your episode. Spotify and Apple Podcasts display clickable chapter links when timestamps are included.
How long should podcast show notes be? ▾
For SEO, aim for 300–500 words minimum. Include the guest name, key topics, timestamps, and resource links. Longer show notes with a partial or full transcript can rank significantly better in search engines.
